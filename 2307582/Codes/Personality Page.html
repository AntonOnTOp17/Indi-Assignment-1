Reading

   I believe one of my main hobbies as of late has been reading. Books have been a cornerstone of my academic growth, expanding my knowledge beyond the classroom and deepening my understanding of various subjects. They have sharpened my critical thinking, broadened my perspective, and fueled my curiosity, helping me develop the skills necessary to excel academically.

   I have never been a fan of studying and always ended up finding other sources of leisure to endulge in but I truly believe that reading helped me to still perform well academically. I sat my CSEC examinations last year July and I vividly remember my mom worrying because I wasn't studying (In the same manner from when I sat GSAT all those years ago). However I wasn't worried, I was calm because I began reading "The 48 Laws of Power" by Robert Greene at the time and simply just did my exams without a worry.   I always say to myself that if I were to have spent my time studying and put pressure on myself instead doing something I enjoyed, I would not have achieved the results I did. Reading serves as my personal escape from the pressures and also helps to clear my mind of all things unneeded at the moment. Reading helps me relax by allowing me to escape into different worlds, shift my focus, and unwind from daily stress. It calms my mind, promotes a sense of peace, and offers a mental break, leaving me refreshed and recharged. I'll end this with a quote from J.K. Rowling which states If you don't like to read, You Haven't found the right book.




My Hero: Escanor

  I don't think I have anybody I could say I view as a hero but a fictional character's personality and idealogy I found really cool is that of Escanor's. Escanor is the lion sin of pride from the popular anime called "The Seven Deadly Sins".His entire persona is centered around being egotistical and over-confident regardless of the situation at hand which leads to intresting dialogue and a sense of hype as a viewer. The quote that truly made me a fan of his character was stated while he was battling a demon who turns anyone who says a lie in his vicinity to stone. The quote states:


"I am a human but,
I'm also the one that stands at the pinnacle of all races."



  This simple quote was truly so surreal to me when I first watched the episode it aired in and even motivated me to begin reading the manga of which the anime was adapted off. To think that a mere human would face a being of a more powerful race and say something so boisterous in a situation where he cannot lie and still leave unscathed.
  I chose Escanor due to how his unwavering confidence can be used as motivation in real world to not falter in the face of adversity.A mindset which believes defeat is not an option will never truly be defeated. Even in my academics I try to always be confident going into any exam or into any assignment and just focus on doing my best instead of being the best.
